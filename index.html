03 / Research & Projects

Selected Work

Paper · 2025

Determinants of the Early-Exercise Premium for American Puts

Led a 3-person team at USC investigating what drives the premium of American-style put options over their European counterparts in the SPY options chain. Used Bloomberg Terminal data, a Monte Carlo simulation framework (1,000 antithetic variate pairs), and GPU-accelerated computation (CuPy) to infer the latent early-exercise premium — a variable that cannot be directly observed in markets. Regressed ln(EEP) on time-to-maturity and implied volatility using an ARMA(3,0,3) model to account for autocorrelation.

Interactive Research Findings

SPY put · Strike $560 · Exp. June 20, 2025 · Bloomberg Terminal

EEP bounds for a single option chain: SPY put, strike $560, expiring June 20, 2025. 150 daily observations (t=1–241) sourced from Bloomberg Terminal. EEPmax and EEPmin are derived from put-call parity bounds adjusted for dividends and bid-ask spreads. The corridor narrows as expiration approaches — confirming the paper's central finding.

Mean coefficient estimates across 1,000 Monte Carlo replications (ARMA(3,0,3) on ln(EEP)). Both significant at p < 0.001. βT avg t-stat ≈ 44.1  |  βivM avg t-stat ≈ 15.7.
